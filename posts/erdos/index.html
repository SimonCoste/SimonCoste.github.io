The spectrum of directed random graphs: global aspects

A directed Erdos-Rényi graph is the simplest of directed random graphs: you put nnn nodes, and each one of the n2n^2n2 potential edges (including self-loops) is put independently with probability ppp. The resulting graph is GnG_nGn​, and we'll note G(n,p)\mathcal{G}(n,p)G(n,p) its probability distribution, even though this notation is usually kept for undirected graphs.

The mean number of edges in GnG_nGn​ is clearly n2pn^2 pn2p. When p=d/np=d/np=d/n, this is ndndnd, a regime called sparse because the mean number of edges has the same order as the number of vertices, as opposed to dense regimes where ppp does not depend on nnn, and the mean number of edges has order n2n^2n2.

The spectrum

The adjacency matrix of GnG_nGn​ is thus an n×nn \times nn×n matrix filled with zeros (a lot) and ones (a few), and it is not symmetric nor normal in any way. Consequently, its nnn eigenalues are complex numbers, say

Λ={{λ1,…,λn}}.\Lambda = \{\{ \lambda_1, \dotsc, \lambda_n \} \} .Λ={{λ1​,…,λn​}}.

The questions

There are not so much results on the properties of μ\muμ in the sparse regime and when nnn is large. There are results when the underlying graph is undirected ; there are results when ddd goes to ∞\infty∞ with nnn ; but otherwise, there is almost nothing.
