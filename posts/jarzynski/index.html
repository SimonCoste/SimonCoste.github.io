The Jarzynski estimator

March 2022

We have a family of potentials VtV_tVt​ on Rn\mathbb{R}^nRn, evolving with time, and their associated Gibbs measures ptp_tpt​, ie

pt(x)=e−Vt(x)ZtwhereZt=∫Rne−Vt(x)dx.\begin{aligned} &p_t(x) = \frac{e^{-V_t(x)}}{Z_t}&& \text{where}&& Z_t = \int_{\mathbb{R}^n} e^{-V_t(x)}dx.\end{aligned}​pt​(x)=Zt​e−Vt​(x)​​​where​​Zt​=∫Rn​e−Vt​(x)dx.​

The goal is to compute, at least numerically, the partition functions ZtZ_tZt​ or equivalently the free energy Ft=−log⁡ZtF_t = - \log Z_tFt​=−logZt​. In many problems, the family of potentials (Vt)(V_t)(Vt​) connects an unknown potential V0V_0V0​, for which we would like to compute F0F_0F0​, and another potential V1V_1V1​ for which F1F_1F1​ is known.

An elegant way of estimating these free energies uses the Jarzynski identity, discovered in 1996. It is based on the Langevin dynamics associated with the evolving potential VtV_tVt​:

dXt=−∇Vt(Xt)dt+2dWtdX_t = - \nabla V_t(X_t)dt + \sqrt{2}dW_t dXt​=−∇Vt​(Xt​)dt+2​dWt​

with (Wt)(W_t)(Wt​) a Brownian motion. Upon standard conditions on VtV_tVt​, this system – a standard Stochastic Differential Equation – has a solution. Note that the distribution of XtX_tXt​ is not ptp_tpt​ in general. However, there is a remarkable identity relating the distribution of XtX_tXt​ with ptp_tpt​.

Jarzynski's identity states that

E[e−∫0tV˙s(Xs)−F˙sds]=1.\mathbf{E}[e^{-\int_0^t \dot{V}_s(X_s) - \dot{F}_sds}] = 1. E[e−∫0t​V˙s​(Xs​)−F˙s​ds]=1.

Here and after, the dot in V˙t\dot{V}_tV˙t​ or F˙t\dot{F}_tF˙t​ always represents a derivative with respect to time.

The consequence of this identity is that

log⁡E[e−∫0tV˙s(Xs)ds]=Ft−F0, \log \mathbf{E}[e^{-\int_0^t \dot{V}_s(X_s)ds}] = F_t - F_0, logE[e−∫0t​V˙s​(Xs​)ds]=Ft​−F0​,

giving rise to a natural estimator for the free energy difference Ft−F0F_t-F_0Ft​−F0​: one samples many paths from the dynamics (1), then computes the path integral wt=∫0tV˙s(Xs)dsw_t = \int_0^t \dot{V}_s(X_s)dswt​=∫0t​V˙s​(Xs​)ds along each path, and then average the values of e−wte^{-w_t}e−wt​ and take the log.

A proof of Jarzynski's identity

Overview

In general, exponential of paths integrals are not easy to study. The classical way is to use the Feynman-Kac representation of PDEs, but this proof seems quite involved; instead, there is a nice trick shown to me by Eric Vanden-Eijnden. The trick is as follows: if ρt(x,y)\rho_t(x,y)ρt​(x,y) is the probability density of the system (Xt,e−∫0tV˙s(Xs)−F˙sds)(X_t, e^{-\int_0^t \dot{V}_s(X_s) - \dot{F}_sds})(Xt​,e−∫0t​V˙s​(Xs​)−F˙s​ds) at ttt, then

  • clearly, we want to compute ∫uρt(x,u)dudx\int u \rho_t(x,u)dudx∫uρt​(x,u)dudx,

  • the quantity at(x)=∫uρt(x,u)dua_t(x) = \int u \rho_t(x,u)duat​(x)=∫uρt​(x,u)du solves an explicit equation,

  • and from this equation we can see that at(x)=e−Vt(x)/Zt=pt(x)a_t(x) = e^{-V_t(x)}/Z_t = p_t(x)at​(x)=e−Vt​(x)/Zt​=pt​(x).
