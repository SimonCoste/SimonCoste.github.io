Zipf

Zipf's law is one of the most famous heavy-tailed distributions "from the real world".

Scale-free networks

Real-world graphs often have heavy-tailed degree sequences. A very beautiful explanation of this phenomenon lies in the famous scale-free property of preferential attachment mechanisms. In PA models, new elements (people, requests, molecules) arrive at each time step; when a new element arrives, it connects to (say) mmm older elements, but it favors elements which alrealy have many connections. Remco van der Hofstadts's book has a whole chapter explaining why the degree of elements in such a model are asymptotically heavy-tailed.
